Median HH Income VS Activity Levels


Analysis


This graph shows the realationship between Median Household Income for the year and the percent of individuals that engaged in Physical Activity in the past month. There seems to be a decent correlation (~.63) between Median Household Income and Physical Activity levels. From the graph, we can gather that areas that have a fairly high Median Income tend to have a high physical activity level. Conversely, states in the Deep South (MS, AR, AL, LA) appear to have among the lowest incomes and the lowest activity levels. Things that may have a hidden impact on this analysis could be when the individuals were polled about activity levels - winter months would tend to have a lower activity level (sans areas that may be geared more towards winter sports) while summer would tend to have a higher activity level. Additionally, the generalization of what constitutes "Physical Activity" could lead to an error in sampling among those polled.
